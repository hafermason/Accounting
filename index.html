🧠 Think of it like this:

You plan what to sell → figure out what to make → determine what resources you need → track the cash → summarize everything in financial statements.

🏭 Production Budget

Budgeted unit sales
+ Desired ending finished goods inventory
= Total needs
− Beginning finished goods inventory
= Required production in units

Example: Parwin Corporation (Q4)

Plans to sell: 43,000 units | Begin inv: 18,000 | Desired ending inv: 19,000

Budgeted sales             43,000
+ Desired ending inventory   19,000
= Total needs                62,000
− Beginning inventory       (18,000)
= Required production       44,000 units ✅

Example: Down Under Products (Q7)

Ending inventory = 15% of FOLLOWING month's sales. Beginning inventory (March 31) = 13,200 units.

🏦 Cash Budget

Beginning cash balance
+ Cash receipts (collections)
= Total cash available
− Cash disbursements
= Excess (deficiency) of cash

FINANCING:
+ Borrowings − Repayments − Interest
= Ending cash balance
⚠️ Key Rules for Borrowing:
1. Must maintain a MINIMUM cash balance
2. Borrow at BEGINNING of quarter
3. Repay at END of quarter
4. Interest = Amount × Rate × Time

Retained Earnings (Q5)

Beginning retained earnings
+ Net income
− Dividends
= Ending retained earnings
⚠️ Watch out! Net income is ADDED. Dividends are SUBTRACTED. Never the opposite!

⚖️ Budgeted Balance Sheet

ASSETS = LIABILITIES + STOCKHOLDERS' EQUITY

Example: Mecca Copy (Q14)

Beginning RE: $27,000 | Net income: $16,600 | Dividends: $3,100

📝 Quick Reference: Key Formulas

🏭 Production

Sales + Desired Ending FG − Beginning FG = Production

📦 Direct Materials

Production Needs + Desired Ending RM − Beginning RM = Purchases

👷 Direct Labor

Units × Hours/Unit × Rate/Hour = Total DL Cost

⚙️ MOH Cash

Total MOH − Depreciation = Cash Disbursements

💼 S&A Cash

Total S&A − Depreciation = Cash Disbursements

💵 Cash Collections

Track % collected by month from each month's sales

🏦 Cash Budget

Begin + Receipts − Disbursements ± Financing = Ending

📋 Retained Earnings

Beginning RE + Net Income − Dividends = Ending RE

🚫 Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Production Budget: Don't forget to ADD ending inventory and SUBTRACT beginning inventory
  2. Materials Budget: Calculate ending inventory based on PRODUCTION needs, not sales
  3. Cash Collections: Count the correct months — "month following sale" means one month later
  4. Cash Budget: Borrow ENOUGH to meet minimum balance, not just to get to zero
  5. Interest: Calculate based on time borrowed (Q1→Q2 = 1 quarter)
  6. Retained Earnings: Net income is ADDED, dividends are SUBTRACTED (never the opposite)
  7. Balance Sheet: Assets must equal Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity

🛁 Study Tip: Inventory Flow

                    ↓ What goes IN
                [=============]
                [     TUB     ]  ← What you HAVE
                [=============]
                    ↓ What goes OUT

Formula: Beginning + Additions − Usage = Ending

Rearranged: Additions = Usage + Ending − Beginning

This logic applies to:

• Finished goods (production in, sales out)
• Raw materials (purchases in, production out)
• Cash (receipts in, disbursements out)
