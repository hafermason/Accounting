# Module 10 Study Guide: Master Budgeting

## Overview
The master budget is a comprehensive financial plan that coordinates all the individual budgets of an organization. Understanding the **sequence** and **formulas** is critical.

---

## 1. THE BUDGETING SEQUENCE (Memorize This!)

```
Sales Budget (STARTING POINT - always first!)
        ↓
Production Budget (how many units to make)
        ↓
    ┌───┴───┬───────────┐
    ↓       ↓           ↓
Direct   Direct    Manufacturing
Materials Labor     Overhead Budget
Budget   Budget
    └───┬───┴───────────┘
        ↓
Selling & Administrative Expense Budget
        ↓
Cash Budget (collections, disbursements, financing)
        ↓
Budgeted Income Statement
        ↓
Budgeted Balance Sheet
```

**Key Concept:** The sales forecast/sales budget is ALWAYS the starting point because everything else depends on how much you plan to sell.

---

## 2. PRODUCTION BUDGET

### The Formula (MEMORIZE):
```
  Budgeted unit sales
+ Desired ending finished goods inventory
= Total needs
- Beginning finished goods inventory
= Required production in units
```

### Example: Parwin Corporation (Question 4)
- Plans to sell: 43,000 units in August
- Beginning inventory: 18,000 units
- Desired ending inventory: 19,000 units

**Solution:**
```
  Budgeted sales                43,000
+ Desired ending inventory      19,000
= Total needs                   62,000
- Beginning inventory          (18,000)
= Required production           44,000 units ← ANSWER
```

### Example: Down Under Products (Question 7)
**Given:**
| Month | Unit Sales |
|-------|------------|
| April | 88,000 |
| May | 90,000 |
| June | 128,000 |
| July | 99,000 |

- Ending inventory = 15% of FOLLOWING month's sales
- Beginning inventory (March 31) = 13,200 units

**Solution:**
| | April | May | June |
|---|---|---|---|
| Budgeted unit sales | 88,000 | 90,000 | 128,000 |
| + Desired ending inventory | 13,500* | 19,200** | 14,850*** |
| = Total needs | 101,500 | 109,200 | 142,850 |
| - Beginning inventory | (13,200) | (13,500) | (19,200) |
| **Required production** | **88,300** | **95,700** | **123,650** |

*April ending = 90,000 × 15% = 13,500
**May ending = 128,000 × 15% = 19,200
***June ending = 99,000 × 15% = 14,850

## 3. DIRECT MATERIALS BUDGET

### The Formula (MEMORIZE):
```
  Raw materials needed for production (units × material per unit)
+ Desired ending raw materials inventory
= Total raw materials needed
- Beginning raw materials inventory
= Raw materials to be PURCHASED
```

**Key:** Required purchases = Needs + Desired Ending - Beginning Inventory

### Example: Mink Caress - Musk Oil (Question 8)
**Given:**
- 3 grams of musk oil per bottle
- Cost: $1.50 per gram
- Ending inventory = 20% of NEXT quarter's production needs
- Beginning inventory = 36,000 grams

| Quarter | Production (bottles) |
|---------|---------------------|
| Year 2 Q1 | 60,000 |
| Year 2 Q2 | 90,000 |
| Year 2 Q3 | 150,000 |
| Year 2 Q4 | 100,000 |
| Year 3 Q1 | 70,000 |

**Solution for Year 2:**

| | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Production in bottles | 60,000 | 90,000 | 150,000 | 100,000 |
| × Grams per bottle | × 3 | × 3 | × 3 | × 3 |
| **Materials needed for production** | 180,000 | 270,000 | 450,000 | 300,000 |
| + Desired ending inventory* | 54,000 | 90,000 | 60,000 | 42,000 |
| = Total needs | 234,000 | 360,000 | 510,000 | 342,000 |
| - Beginning inventory | (36,000) | (54,000) | (90,000) | (60,000) |
| **Materials to purchase (grams)** | **198,000** | **306,000** | **420,000** | **282,000** |
| × Cost per gram | × $1.50 | × $1.50 | × $1.50 | × $1.50 |
| **Cost of materials purchased** | **$297,000** | **$459,000** | **$630,000** | **$423,000** |

*Ending inventory calculations:
- Q1 ending = Q2 production needs × 20% = 270,000 × 20% = 54,000
- Q2 ending = Q3 production needs × 20% = 450,000 × 20% = 90,000
- Q3 ending = Q4 production needs × 20% = 300,000 × 20% = 60,000
- Q4 ending = Y3Q1 production needs × 20% = (70,000 × 3) × 20% = 42,000

## 4. DIRECT LABOR BUDGET

### The Formula:
```
  Units to be produced
× Direct labor hours per unit
= Total direct labor hours needed
× Direct labor cost per hour
= Total direct labor cost
```

### Example: Rordan Corporation (Question 9)
**Given:**
- 0.75 direct labor hours per unit
- $14.00 per hour

| Quarter | Units to Produce |
|---------|-----------------|
| Q1 | 9,800 |
| Q2 | 7,500 |
| Q3 | 7,900 |
| Q4 | 10,200 |

**Solution:**
| | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Units to be produced | 9,800 | 7,500 | 7,900 | 10,200 |
| × DL hours per unit | × 0.75 | × 0.75 | × 0.75 | × 0.75 |

| × Cost per hour | × $14.00 | × $14.00 | × $14.00 | × $14.00 |
| **Total direct labor cost** | **$102,900** | **$78,750** | **$82,950** | **$107,100** |

## 5. MANUFACTURING OVERHEAD BUDGET

### The Formula:
```
  Budgeted direct labor hours
× Variable manufacturing overhead rate
= Variable manufacturing overhead

+ Fixed manufacturing overhead
= Total manufacturing overhead

- Depreciation (noncash)
= Cash disbursements for manufacturing overhead
```

**Given:**

| Quarter | Budgeted DL Hours |
|---------|------------------|
| Q1 | 12,000 |
| Q2 | 10,200 |
| Q3 | 10,500 |
| Q4 | 11,300 |

**Solution:**
| | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Budgeted DL hours | 12,000 | 10,200 | 10,500 | 11,300 |
| × Variable MOH rate | × $7.00 | × $7.00 | × $7.00 | × $7.00 |
| **Variable MOH** | **$84,000** | **$71,400** | **$73,500** | **$79,100** |
| + Fixed MOH | 88,000 | 88,000 | 88,000 | 88,000 |
| **Total MOH** | **$172,000** | **$159,400** | **$161,500** | **$167,100** |
| - Depreciation | (22,000) | (22,000) | (22,000) | (22,000) |

## 7. SCHEDULE OF EXPECTED CASH COLLECTIONS

### Key Concept:
Sales are often made on credit, so cash isn't collected immediately. You need to track WHEN cash comes in based on the collection pattern.

### Example: Pitkin Corporation (Question 3)
**Given:**
- 70% collected in month of sale
- 20% collected in month following sale
- 10% collected in second month following sale

| Month | Budgeted Sales |
|-------|---------------|
| January | $200,000 |
| February | $300,000 |
| March | $350,000 |
| April | $250,000 |

**What are total cash collections in April?**

Cash collected in April comes from:
- April sales: $250,000 × 70% = $175,000
- March sales: $350,000 × 20% = $70,000
- February sales: $300,000 × 10% = $30,000
- **Total April collections = $275,000**

## 8. CASH BUDGET

### The Structure:
```
Beginning cash balance
+ Cash receipts (collections)
= Total cash available

- Cash disbursements
= Excess (deficiency) of cash

FINANCING:
+ Borrowings
- Repayments
- Interest
= Ending cash balance
```

### Key Rules for Borrowing Problems:
1. Company must maintain a MINIMUM cash balance
2. Borrow at BEGINNING of quarter (if needed)
3. Repay at END of quarter (when excess cash available)
4. Interest is on the AMOUNT borrowed × rate × TIME

### Example: Garden Depot (Question 12)
**Given:**
- Beginning cash balance: $20,000
- Minimum cash balance required: $10,000
- Quarterly interest rate: 3%
- Borrow at beginning of quarter, repay at end

| Quarter | Cash Receipts | Cash Disbursements |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Q1 | $350,000 | $393,000 |
| Q2 | $470,000 | $363,000 |
| Q3 | $400,000 | $353,000 |
| Q4 | $420,000 | $373,000 |

**Solution:**

| | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beginning cash balance | $20,000 | $10,000 | $10,000 | $10,000 |
| + Total cash receipts | 350,000 | 470,000 | 400,000 | 420,000 |
| **Total cash available** | **$370,000** | **$480,000** | **$410,000** | **$430,000** |
| - Total cash disbursements | (393,000) | (363,000) | (353,000) | (373,000) |
| **Excess (deficiency)** | **($23,000)** | **$117,000** | **$57,000** | **$57,000** |
| **Financing:** | | | | |
| Borrowings* | 33,000 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Repayments | 0 | (33,000) | 0 | 0 |
| Interest** | 0 | (990) | 0 | 0 |
| **Total financing** | **$33,000** | **($33,990)** | **$0** | **$0** |
| **Ending cash balance** | **$10,000** | **$83,010** | **$57,000** | **$57,000** |

*Q1 borrowing: Need to get from ($23,000) to $10,000 minimum = $33,000 needed
**Interest: $33,000 × 3% × 1 quarter = $990 (borrowed Q1, repaid Q2 = 1 quarter)

## 10. RETAINED EARNINGS (Question 5)

### The Formula (MEMORIZE):
```
Beginning retained earnings
+ Net income
- Dividends
= Ending retained earnings
```

**This is the CORRECT formula!** Watch out for trick answers that:
- Subtract net income (wrong - net income ADDS to RE)
- Add dividends (wrong - dividends REDUCE RE)

## 11. BUDGETED BALANCE SHEET

### Structure:
**ASSETS**
- Current assets (Cash, A/R, Inventory, Supplies)
- Plant & equipment (Gross - Accumulated depreciation = Net)
- Total assets

**LIABILITIES & STOCKHOLDERS' EQUITY**
- Current liabilities (A/P, etc.)
- Stockholders' equity (Common stock + Retained earnings)
- Total liabilities and stockholders' equity

### Example: Mecca Copy (Question 14)
**Given:**
| Account | Ending Balance |
|---------|---------------|
| Cash | ? |
| Accounts receivable | $9,100 |
| Supplies inventory | $2,600 |
| Equipment | $39,000 |
| Accumulated depreciation | $15,800 |
| Accounts payable | $2,800 |
| Common stock | $5,000 |
| Retained earnings | ? |

Additional info:
- Beginning retained earnings: $27,000
- Net income budgeted: $16,600
- Dividends budgeted: $3,100

**Step 1: Calculate Retained Earnings**
```
Beginning RE          $27,000
+ Net income          16,600
- Dividends           (3,100)
= Ending RE          $40,500
```

**Step 2: Calculate Cash (plug figure)**
Total L&SE = A/P + Common Stock + RE = $2,800 + $5,000 + $40,500 = $48,300
Total Assets must equal $48,300

Plant & equipment, net = $39,000 - $15,800 = $23,200
Other current assets = $9,100 + $2,600 = $11,700
Cash = $48,300 - $23,200 - $11,700 = $13,400

**Budgeted Balance Sheet:**

| **ASSETS** | |
|---|---|
| Current assets: | |
| Cash | $13,400 |
| Accounts receivable | 9,100 |
| Supplies inventory | 2,600 |
| **Total current assets** | **$25,100** |
| Plant and equipment: | |
| Equipment | $39,000 |
| Less: Accumulated depreciation | (15,800) |
| **Plant and equipment, net** | **$23,200** |
| **Total assets** | **$48,300** |
| | |
| **LIABILITIES AND STOCKHOLDERS' EQUITY** | |
| Current liabilities: | |
| Accounts payable | $2,800 |
| Stockholders' equity: | |
| Common stock | $5,000 |
| Retained earnings | 40,500 |
| **Total stockholders' equity** | **$45,500** |
| **Total liabilities and stockholders' equity** | **$48,300** |

## 12. PEARL PRODUCTS - COMBINED EXAMPLE (Question 15)

This question combines Production Budget AND Direct Materials Budget.

**Given:**
- 3 cc of solvent H300 per unit of Supermix
- Ending FG inventory = 2,000 units + 20% of next month's sales
- Ending raw materials = 50% of next month's production needs
- June 30 FG inventory: 10,600 units
- June 30 raw materials inventory: 66,000 cc

| Month | Budgeted Unit Sales |
|-------|-------------------|
| July | 43,000 |
| August | 48,000 |
| September | 58,000 |
| October | 38,000 |
| November | 28,000 |
| December | 18,000 |

**Part 1: Production Budget (July-October)**

| | July | Aug | Sept | Oct |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Budgeted unit sales | 43,000 | 48,000 | 58,000 | 38,000 |
| + Desired ending FG inventory* | 11,600 | 13,600 | 9,600 | 7,600 |
| = Total needs | 54,600 | 61,600 | 67,600 | 45,600 |
| - Beginning FG inventory | (10,600) | (11,600) | (13,600) | (9,600) |
| **Required production** | **44,000** | **50,000** | **54,000** | **36,000** |

*Ending FG = 2,000 + (next month's sales × 20%)
- July ending = 2,000 + (48,000 × 20%) = 11,600
- Aug ending = 2,000 + (58,000 × 20%) = 13,600
- Sept ending = 2,000 + (38,000 × 20%) = 9,600
- Oct ending = 2,000 + (28,000 × 20%) = 7,600

**Part 2: Direct Materials Budget (July-September)**

First, calculate October production needs for Sept ending inventory:
Oct production = 36,000 units × 3 cc = 108,000 cc needed

| | July | Aug | Sept |
|---|---|---|---|
| Required production (units) | 44,000 | 50,000 | 54,000 |
| × Materials per unit (cc) | × 3 | × 3 | × 3 |
| **Materials for production** | 132,000 | 150,000 | 162,000 |
| + Desired ending RM inventory* | 75,000 | 81,000 | 54,000 |
| = Total RM needs | 207,000 | 231,000 | 216,000 |
| - Beginning RM inventory | (66,000) | (75,000) | (81,000) |
| **Materials to purchase (cc)** | **141,000** | **156,000** | **135,000** |

*Ending RM = 50% of next month's production needs
- July ending = 150,000 × 50% = 75,000
- Aug ending = 162,000 × 50% = 81,000
- Sept ending = 108,000 × 50% = 54,000

## QUICK REFERENCE: KEY FORMULAS

| Budget Type | Formula |
|-------------|---------|
| **Production** | Sales + Desired Ending FG - Beginning FG = Production |
| **Direct Materials** | Production Needs + Desired Ending RM - Beginning RM = Purchases |
| **Direct Labor** | Units × Hours/Unit × Rate/Hour = Total DL Cost |
| **MOH Cash** | Total MOH - Depreciation = Cash Disbursements |
| **S&A Cash** | Total S&A - Depreciation = Cash Disbursements |
| **Cash Collections** | Track % collected by month from each month's sales |
| **Cash Budget** | Begin + Receipts - Disbursements ± Financing = Ending |
| **Retained Earnings** | Beginning RE + Net Income - Dividends = Ending RE |

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Production Budget:** Don't forget to ADD ending inventory and SUBTRACT beginning inventory
2. **Materials Budget:** Calculate ending inventory based on PRODUCTION needs, not sales
3. **Cash Collections:** Count the correct months - "month following sale" means one month later
4. **Cash Budget:** Borrow ENOUGH to meet minimum balance, not just to get to zero
5. **Interest:** Calculate based on time borrowed (if repaid in Q2 and borrowed in Q1, that's 1 quarter)
6. **Retained Earnings:** Net income is ADDED, dividends are SUBTRACTED (never the opposite)
7. **Balance Sheet:** Assets must equal Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity (use this to check/find missing values)

## STUDY TIP: Inventory Flow

Think of inventory like a bathtub:
```
                    ↓ What goes IN
                [=========]
                [  TUB    ]  ← What you HAVE
                [=========]
                    ↓ What goes OUT (used)
```

**Formula:** Beginning + Additions - Usage = Ending
**Rearranged:** Additions = Usage + Ending - Beginning

This logic applies to:
- Finished goods (production in, sales out)
- Raw materials (purchases in, production out)
- Cash (receipts in, disbursements out)
